Introduction to Math!

Welcome to our math segment! Here we are going to learn about using math ideas in real life. Math can be useful outside the classroom, and you will likely encounter in your everday life. Below we will quiz you on your ability to use your math skills in the real world. But first, we will be reviewing some key concepts.

Basic counting skills are very important! Be sure you are familiar with your basic numberline before continuing!

In your day to day life you will encounter addition in many places! To the right is an addition chart. If you choose a number on the top and a number on the left, the table tells you the total when those numbers are added together. Just see where the top number's column, and left side number's row intersect, and there is the answer!

You may not think fractions would ever show up in life outside math class, but you'd be mistaken! Fractions help us understand how to divide up a whole thing easily and fairly. Make sure you are familiar with how to write and read fractions.

Introduction to the Universe!



Welcome to our science section! In this page of the science section, we are learning about outer space! We will be covering our solar system in the section below. The universe outside our little planet, is massive and infinitely growing. Get ready to learn things that are out of this world!




When you look up at the night sky, do you ever wonder what you are seeing? It's the Milky Way! Our home planet Earth, is located in the galaxy known as the Milky Way. A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems, all held together by gravity. All the stars in the sky you see, are the other stars in the Milky Way. Pretty massive right? But there is much more to the universe than the Milky Way. Some scientists think there could be as many as one hundred billion galaxies in the universe.

Earth orbits the Sun, which is at the center of our Solar System. Our solar system consists of our star, the Sun, and everything bound to it by gravity – the planets Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune; dwarf planets such as Pluto; dozens of moons; and millions of asteroids, comets, and meteoroids.
